Saab's History

In 1945, the Swedish Aerospace and defence company, Saab, designed their first automobile. in 1949 they released the Saab 92, They continued to have success and in 1968 merged with Scania-Vabis, a decade later they released their best selling model, The 900. This car had many quirks and features that enticed buyers, selling a total of ~909,000 units. In 1989, Saab-Scania was restructured and General Motors took a 50% stake the company. during this period Saab released the 9-3 and the 9-5 models, some of the more well known Saab cars. In 2010 GM decided to sell Saab, there were a few options, inlcuding Koenigsegg, a company now known for some of the fastest cars in the world, like the Agera, Regera, Jesko, and others. However, Spyker Cars was the final buyer. After many years of making innovative cars and building their brand, they eventually filed for bankruptcy in 2011, and by June of 2012, they had sold all of factories and manufacturing rights to the new company NEVS (National Electric Vehicle Sweden). NEVS tried to start making the Saab cars again but lost the rights to the Saab name, still owned by the Saab Aerospace company that is still alive today, and instead began making electric cars based on the 9-3 model, but under their own company name. Saab has a rich history of innovation in quality, speed, comfort, efficiency, safety, and more. Unfortuntely they were unable to grow enough of a following and after a few questionable years, were forced into bankruptcy.

My Saab

My very first car was a 2008 9-3, It isn't the fastest car, or the most luxurious. But it had comfortable seats, a funny cupholder that always made for a fun party trick, a great turbo sound, a manual transmission, and many features that many low to mid tier cars today don't even have. I've grown to love that car and will always feel a special attachment to the Saab brand.
